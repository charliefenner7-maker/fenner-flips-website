## Email 3 — Day 3 (the most common worry)

**Subject:** "Are you going to lowball me?"

{{first_name}},

This is the question we hear most often, so I want to answer it head-on.

Honest answer: our offer is almost always lower than what a fully renovated, perfectly staged version of your house would sell for on MLS.

That's the trade. You don't do the repairs. You don't pay the 5–6% in agent commissions. You don't carry the house for 30–60 days while it sits on market. You don't pay closing costs (we do). You don't haggle through the inspection period.

Once you back all of that out of an MLS sale price, the gap between "list it" and "sell to us" is usually smaller than people think. Sometimes the numbers come out almost identical — but you walk away three months sooner with zero stress.

The only way to know which one wins for your house is to see the actual numbers. We give those numbers free, in writing, with no obligation.

If you want to see what your house would actually look like as an offer, just hit reply with the address. I'll have a number back to you in 24 hours.

— Charlie

## Email 5 — Day 8 (the soft pitch)

**Subject:** Should you sell now or wait?

{{first_name}},

I'm going to do something most "real estate investors" won't do: tell you when *not* to sell to us.

If your house is in good shape, you have plenty of time, and your priority is getting the highest possible number — list it with an agent. That's the right move. We'd actually recommend a few we trust.

But if any of these are true, a direct sale is worth a real conversation:

- The house needs more repairs than you want to deal with
- You inherited it and don't want to manage it from out of state
- You're moving and need a known closing date
- You're behind on payments or staring down foreclosure
- You've got tenants you're done managing
- You just want to be done

If you check any of those boxes, hit reply. We'll get on a 15-minute call, look at what makes sense for your specific situation, and you'll have an answer either way.

No charge. No pressure. No obligation.
