Chapter 2

The 5 hidden costs of an MLS listing most agents won't mention

Listing with an agent is the right call for plenty of homeowners. But the price tag is rarely what people think. Here's the math we walk every seller through.

1Agent commissions: 5–6% of sale price

On a $400,000 Nashville home, that's $20,000–$24,000 off the top — split between your agent and the buyer's agent. Doesn't matter how good the offer is.

2Pre-listing repairs and prep

Most agents will hand you a punch list before they list: paint, flooring, kitchen touch-ups, landscaping, decluttering, sometimes staging. We've seen sellers spend $8,000–$25,000 here. If money is already tight, this is the trap.

3Carrying costs while it sits

The Nashville market has been good, but houses still average 30–60 days on market once listed — and that's after the prep. Mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities, and lawn care during that window can run $2,500–$5,000/month.

4Inspection-period concessions

Buyer goes under contract, sends an inspector, comes back asking for $4,000 in roof work or $2,000 toward HVAC. You either pay it or lose the deal and re-list.

5Closing costs you didn't know were yours

Title insurance, transfer tax, prorated property tax, attorney fees. These add another 1–3% of sale price the agent won't mention until you're at the closing table.

Add it up. On a $400k Nashville home, the gap between your gross sale price and the check you actually walk away with can easily be $40,000–$70,000. That's the real cost of "list it and see."

Chapter 3

4 situations where a direct sale almost always wins

We're not going to tell you a direct sale is right for everyone. It's not. But here are the four situations where we've watched sellers come out ahead almost every single time:

1. Inherited property you don't live in

Probate fees. Out-of-state property management. The estate is paying taxes, utilities, and HOA on a house nobody is using. Selling fast — even at a slight discount — usually beats six more months of carrying costs and family stress.

2. Foreclosure timeline

If a notice of sale is on the books, every week matters. We can usually close fast enough to pay the loan off before the auction date — which protects your credit. Most agent listings can't move that quickly.

3. Major repairs the bank won't lend on

Foundation issues, fire damage, roof, mold, knob-and-tube wiring — if the house can't pass an FHA or conventional inspection, your buyer pool through MLS shrinks to investors only. At that point, going direct to an investor cuts out the middlemen and the showings.

4. You need certainty, not the highest possible number

Job relocation. Divorce. Health issue. New baby. Sometimes the right move isn't getting the best price — it's getting a closing date you can plan around. A signed contract with a real buyer beats three months of "we're getting close to an offer."

Chapter 5

The honest answers to the worries we hear most

"Are you going to lowball me?"

The honest answer: our offer will almost always be lower than what a fully renovated, perfectly staged version of your house would sell for on MLS. That's the whole trade — you don't do the work, you don't wait, you don't pay the fees. We're absorbing the risk and the cost. Once you back out commissions, repairs, and carrying costs, the gap is usually smaller than people expect.

"My house isn't pretty enough to show"

That's literally our business model. We bought a house in 37206 last year that hadn't had a kitchen update since 1971. Another in 37212 had a tree growing through the back porch. We don't care about pretty. We care about the lot, the bones, and the numbers.

"What if I'm not sure I want to sell yet?"

Then don't. Get the offer anyway, hold it for two weeks, see how it feels. There's no obligation. Plenty of homeowners we talk to decide to wait — and we still pick up the phone when they call back six months later.

"What if I owe more than the house is worth?"

Tell us. Sometimes we can structure a deal that still works. Sometimes we can't and we'll tell you that too. Either way you'll know more than you do right now.

"What if I have tenants?"

We buy occupied properties regularly. We can either close subject to the existing lease or work with you on a transition plan. Tenant situations don't kill our deals.
